Hello world! Welcome to my blog, this is my first blog post and here I just want to say hello, and expose some of the thoughts I'm having
right now. It's meant to be just a picture of my thoughts at the moment, to serve as a record of this time we're living right now, and how
I'm facing it. So that I wen't back here three years in the future and see how dumb I was.

And because of this, I will write this entire blog myself, by my hands, no AI generation, and no AI review. Every mistake done is only
mine. You're gonna notice this post is about it as well. How we're entering a world where human mediocracy, or genosity, is not something
anymore.

Ok, but what exactly is this post about? It's about AI (sorry). But it's more about the impacts of AI in me, and how I'm living through it.

## where we are

I'm a software engineer, around 5 years of professional experience, bachelor's degree in computer science, technician degree in informatics.
This means at least 9 years dedicated to learning and expressing my value through code. And it's been 2 or 3 months I don't code anymore.

By don't code anymore I mean that I don't write characters to a code editor myself, it's just not the most efficient way to work anymore.
Of course I'm still coding in a sense, and actually one could argue I'm coding much more. I really though that this moment wouldn't happen,
that it was impossible, and I think most of the dev industry had the same vision. But it already fell apart.

Now, the most efficient way to code is to prompt one of the SoTA harnesses like Claude Code, Codex, OpenCode, etc. Use one of the best AI
workflows like compound engineering, GSD, etc. Generate and review the code (and review using AI). And many would say that this is already
behind as well and there's something :alarm: URGENT :alarm: something just released and broke everyhing behind that is much better.

So, to answer where we are: I think we're lost. Every other week a top company CEO say something crazy, a company releases 5 products and
a new model (that does the same as the past onea), thousands of tech gurus/influencers give their take about the latest news that you can't
miss. And the actual truth we're really lost, we really don't know where we're gonna end up after all of this.

## the loss of charm

One of the things that actually make me sad in this new world is that I fell I lost the charm for code/engineering. Everyday on X, youtube
or github I see a new :alartm: urgent :alarm: ground breaking, awesome new tool that just one engineer has broken top tier companies on a
very important field. And that's everyday, all the time, "something great" is built. So, I don't find it awesome anymore. I feel that if I
had the idea, and had spent the tokens, I could have built it as well. Not that it takes away the merit of the builder, but I'm just not
astonished that a human being was able to build that. And it's almost like nothing could make me have the charm a tool now.

A big example it for me is one of the most impactful projects in the agentic AI era: ~~clawdbot~~ ~~moltbot~~ ***openclaw***. Peter Steinberger 
just got it right, he had the exact and correct idea on what the agentic AI should be, and what was missing from the existing agents. But,
I was zero surprised by the technological solution, by the implementation, by the engineering of it. And, it was "agentic engineered" (not vibe
coded) as he said.

And that's even for myself actually, if I build a new awesome project using AI, I haven't built it myself. I just had the correct idea, the
correct workflow, and the enough amount of tokens spent. It's almost as if wasn't me that built that, it's much more robotic and less
though intensive.

After the advent of the agentic engineering I already built stuff that I'm sure that I wasn't able to do before:

* A TUI for playing guitar in C++: [dont-amplify](https://github.com/IgorFroehner/dont-amplify)
* This static blog generator in go: [dont-blog](https://github.com/IgorFroehner/dont-blog)
* A note taking, white board, todo list, + other features in React + Convex: [dont-control](https://github.com/IgorFroehner/dont-control)

And I'm sure who is reading this might have built some other much more interesting stuff (that I won't be surprised at all, but congrats!).
